01

Project Overview

MyGoals is a mobile application designed to provide users with a flexible and personalised solution for interval training. The app allows users to create and customise their own interval timers, tailoring them to their specific workout routines and fitness goals.

By providing a user-friendly interface and robust customisation options, the app empowers individuals to take control of their training and maximise their workout effectiveness.

02

Problem Statement

Traditional interval timer apps lack the flexibility and customisation options necessary to accommodate the diverse needs of users. Many fitness enthusiasts and athletes prefer to follow personalised interval training programs tailored to their specific goals and fitness levels.

However, existing apps often offer pre-set timers that do not align with individual preferences, limiting the user's ability to optimise their workout experience.

Goal 01 — Enable users to create personalised interval timers based on their specific workout routines, goals, and fitness levels.
Goal 02 — Provide a user-friendly and intuitive interface that simplifies creating and managing custom timers.
Goal 03 — Offer a wide range of customisation options — interval duration, rest periods, repetitions, audio feedback, and music app connection.
Goal 04 — Improve the overall workout experience by empowering users to have greater control and flexibility in their interval training.
03

Design Process

Using the Design Thinking approach — Discover, Define, Ideate, and Prototype — the project followed a structured research-to-delivery workflow.

04

User Research

Conducted user research to understand the needs, preferences, and pain points of individuals interested in interval training. A card sorting study with 15 participants provided key findings:

Measurement Preferences — Participants consistently grouped tracking features (distance, pace, history) into a dedicated category, leading to a consolidated "Measurement" section.
Goal Setting — Strong desire for personalised workout goals, reminders, and progress tracking — resulting in an "add a note" feature for in-app goal setting.
Visual Preferences — Clear, intuitive interface with consistent colour schemes and distinguishable visual elements were highly valued.
05

Wireframes &
Prototypes

Translated sketches into graphics, reconsidering interaction elements at each stage. The high-fidelity prototype featured a consistent design system — branding, typography, colour scheme — with intuitive icons guiding users through customisation.

06

What I've Learned

Reflection

Taking on this project was a great experience. I learned about the importance of building a design system and sticking to it, even if the task is relatively simple. More in-person data collection and earlier user flows would have helped avoid spending time on onboarding flows that ended up being unnecessarily long. These learnings shaped my understanding of user-centric design, the value of iteration, and the significance of attention to detail.
